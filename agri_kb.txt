Pesticide spraying should be avoided during rainfall as chemicals get washed away.

Irrigation is not required if rainfall is expected within 24 hours.

Sowing seeds is good when soil is moist but not waterlogged.

High humidity and rain increase fungal disease risk in crops.

Neem oil spray is effective against aphids and whiteflies in dry weather.

Overwatering leads to root rot due to poor soil aeration.

Crop diseases spread faster in wet and humid climate conditions.

Windy conditions are not suitable for pesticide spraying.

Yellow leaves in plants are often due to nitrogen deficiency.
